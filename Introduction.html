Qian Xuesen

Qian Xuesen (December 11, 1911 - October 31, 2009; Hsue-Shen Tsien, formerly known as the English name in the United States, and Qian Xuesen after returning to China), Zhejiang Hangzhou, an internationally renowned Chinese aerodynamicist and system scientist (One of the founders of engineering cybernetics), former U.S. Air Force colonel, senior civil service officer of the Chinese People's Liberation Army, first-class heroic model, academician of the Chinese Academy of Sciences and academician of the Chinese Academy of Engineering. He returned to the United States in 1935 to study in the United States with a formality compensation for Geng son, graduated from Theodor von Carmen in 1936; was sent to Germany in 1945 to investigate German rocket technology in Nazi Germany; 1955 captured in the Korean war air combat The U.S. military pilots exchange back to mainland China. Former MIT and California Institute of Technology professor, is California Polytechnic Jet Propulsion Laboratory and the People's Republic of China Ministry of Defense Institute of the Fifth Institute of Chinese Academy of Sciences and one of the main creators, as the seventh vice president of the Ministry of Machinery Industry The minister and deputy director of the PLA's national defense science and technology committee made a significant contribution to the missile and space programs of both China and the United States [1], winning the honorary titles of "two bombs and one bronze medal" and "national outstanding contribution scientist" The only winner. He was the vice chairman of the 6th, 7th and 8th National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference and ranked as the deputy party and state leaders at the national level.
